            <center><h1> What Is A GT3 Race Car </h1></center>
            <img src="Test_1/GT3_images/2cb8c1104728537.5f69a637480e2.jpg" width="1165" height="600">
            <p> 
                A GT3 car is a high-performance racing vehicle built to FIA regulations for grand tourer racing. 
                Based on production two-door models, these cars feature extensive racing modifications—such as lightweight bodies, intense aerodynamics, 
                and racing-spec brakes—to compete globally in sprint and endurance, often utilizing Balance of Performance (BoP) to ensure close, competitive racing.
            </p>
        </div>

        <div>
            <center><h1> Different Car Manufacturers </h1></center>
            <img src="Test_1/GT3_images/gzghmqdkz1ud1.jpeg" width="1165" height="600">
            <p> 
                Major GT3 car manufacturers producing race-ready machines for championships like GT World Challenge and IMSA include Porsche (911 GT3 R), Ferrari (296 GT3), Lamborghini (Huracán GT3 EVO2), Mercedes-AMG (GT3 EVO), BMW (M4 GT3), Aston Martin (Vantage AMR GT3), McLaren (720S GT3 EVO), and Chevrolet (Corvette Z06 GT3.R). 
                Other manufacturers with homologated cars include Ford (Mustang GT3), Audi (R8 LMS GT3 Evo II), Lexus (RC F GT3), and Acura (NSX GT3 Evo22). 
            </p>
        </div>

        <div>
            <center><h1> Difference Between IMSA And WEC </h1></center>
            <img src="Test_1/GT3_images/GcwvRgpW8AApDni.jpg" width="1165" height="600">
            <p> 
                IMSA (North America) and WEC (Global/FIA) are premier endurance series with shared top-tier machinery (GTP/Hypercar) but differ significantly in operations. 
                IMSA features shorter, incident-packed races with simultaneous fueling/tire changes, while WEC holds longer 6–24 hour races, separates pit crew tasks, and runs a global, 8-round championship. 
            </p>
        </div>
